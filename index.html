From Corporate to Craftsman

Richard "Ricky" Wharton didn't start with scissors in hand. Like many of us, he spent years in the corporate grind, dreaming of something more meaningful.

The turning point? Walking into a traditional barbershop and experiencing true craftsmanship. That moment sparked a passion that would change everything— first for him, now for every client who sits in his chair.

See His Work ↓

Mastering the Art

At Dennison Dahlman, Ricky didn't just learn to cut hair—he learned to read people. Every client became a study in precision, every cut a lesson in perfection.

Five years of honing his craft, building relationships, and developing the philosophy that would define his own shop: one chair, undivided attention, uncompromising quality.

The Single Chair Sanctuary

When Ricky opened Wharton Barbershop, he made a radical choice: one chair. While others chased volume, he chose intimacy. While others rushed, he chose precision.

The result? A loyal following of clients who understand the difference between getting a haircut and experiencing craftsmanship. Some, like Shea, have been coming for over 5 years—following Ricky wherever his journey takes him.
